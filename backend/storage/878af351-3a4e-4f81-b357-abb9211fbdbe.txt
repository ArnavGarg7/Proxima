PRESENTING COMPLAINT

The patient was brought in by his family members due to him experiencing chest pain and shortness of breath for a few days. He also said he was feeling really tired and dizzy on and off. These symptoms were reported to have started gradually.

---

MEDICAL HISTORY

A history of hypertension and Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus has been reported by the patient. He mentioned he takes some medications for both conditions but couldn't exactly remember the names. It was noted by the nursing staff that he seemed somewhat confused about his medication schedule. His father is said to have had a heart attack sometime in his 60s.

A previous episode of chest discomfort was experienced by the patient approximately two years ago, however no formal diagnosis was made at that time and follow-up was not pursued by the patient.

- Pulse was found to be irregular and was somewhere between 88–94 bpm
- SpO2 reading was low, approximately 91% on room air
- Temperature seemed normal

INVESTIGATIONS ORDERED

A number of tests were ordered by the attending physician. These included an ECG, chest X-ray, complete blood count, serum electrolytes, and cardiac enzyme levels. It was also felt by the team that an echocardiogram should probably be done at some point during the admission.

Results are still pending for most of these tests and will be updated when available.

However this has not yet been confirmed and more information is needed before a definitive conclusion can be reached by the team.
